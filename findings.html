Summary of Findings

I feel that the cognitive walkthroughs game me the most opportunity for grow since it allow me to find small details with the content that, if improve, could create a much better user experience. Overall I found that the information on my website was not being properly display. It seem to be the correct information but the design choice of using long descriptive paragraphs was not working really well and actually turned away the user from reading the content at all. Adding to the difficulty of finding the information the user requires. The tites for the different taps were not as clear as I had hoped. I tried hard to keep the tiles short and descriptive, but it seems that this approach was not descriptive enough and often left users wondere the details of the information within each page.

After the cognitive walkthroughs the user stories gave me the most information with regards to the major usability problems of my website. Even though the stories were written using my own perspective and ideas, the short nature of them forced me to reduce my ideas to the basics, which then showed me some shortcomings on the information in my website. I found in many occasions the information in my website is not explicitly stated and the user has to look for their answers. I was fast to realized that my user based seemed unlikely to search for anything on my website, or to even spend more than a couple of seconds looking for the information they need.

While creating the user stories, I was also to notice a design aspect that had nothing to do with the stories themselves. While exploring my website to find information relevant that I could include in the stories, I found that even I was having difficulty navigating the site. I feel that this was mostly do to the little inconsistencies between pages, and how the overall look and feel of the site is not shared between pages, and each pages feels like it stands on its own with outside links to other pages, instead of it all feeling as part of one application.

Incorporation of Findings

The first thing I plan to do is to change the taps names, at this point i feel that it would be best to change the name for both the Home and Contact me tap, and to try to merge the Work experience and the projects tap into one. Ideally I would like to add the contact information to the bottom of the page and to keep the portfolio to two main taps, but do to the difficulty I had setting up a footer for all the pages, I believe it would be best to leave on it’s on tap for right now.

I would also like to add functionality to the contact section, adding links to my email, linked in account, facebook, and github. One functionality that I just saw in class that I personality really appreciate in other websites is the copy to clipboard option for the email. At the moment, I do not know how this feature would be implemented, so I will leave it out the website, but it is a nice feature that I would like to have.

Another important thing is adding subtitles to each one of the pages. I feel that this is necessary to allow the user to better navigate the pages and to easily find the information they need. Adding to this concept, I will be adding a skills section, probably within the experience pages, since I feel that the website does a poor job at showcasing my abilities as a programmer and all the different tool I’m proficient with.

The biggest pain point of the website, and sadly one the I will not be able to fix within the scope of this project is the overall design of the website. As a UX designer I see many shortcomings of the overall functionality of the site and I believe that I know how to solve most of them. Unfortunately, my programming skills and my HTML knowledge does not match the expectations I have for the site. For me to accomplish this goal I will spend winter break experimenting with Bootstrap, enabling to improve the design of my site and also to apply this new knowledge on another class the I know will require me to be able to utilize this tool within others.
